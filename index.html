About Me

I grew up on the Navajo Reservation in northeastern Arizona until the ninth grade. I then went to school for my freshman and sophomore years in Massachusetts. Then I finished high school at Holbrook high school back in Arizona. I enrolled at Arizona State University my freshman year and then moved to Flagstaff where I took more courses. I eventually moved to Mesa and finished up at ASU with a degree in geography. I then went on to work for 21 years with the US Geological Survey in both Flagstaff and Tucson. Working with Geographic Information Systems (GIS) got me in the door but eventuallyI did some front and back-end web development in support of the National Geologic Map Database while working as a member of a team. We used a lot of open-source software and I pretty much got by with a VI editor to make code and relied on my web browser to fix bugs. Eventually I left the Survey and figured I could do so much more in web development if I were to be exposed to the proper tools and make a dedicated attempt to learn.
